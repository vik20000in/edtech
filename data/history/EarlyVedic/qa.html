Early Vedic Period - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is the main source of information for the Early Vedic Period?
Ans: The Rig Veda is the main source of information.
Q2: Who were the Aryans?
Ans: The Aryans were a group of people who arrived in India from Central Asia around 1500 BCE.
Q3: What does the word 'Veda' mean?
Ans: The word 'Veda' means knowledge.
Q4: Name the four Vedas.
Ans: Rig Veda, Sama Veda, Yajur Veda, and Atharva Veda.
Q5: What was the Sapta Sindhu?
Ans: The land of seven rivers where the Aryans first settled. It included the Indus and its tributaries.
Q6: Who was the head of the family in the Early Vedic period?
Ans: The Grihapati or Kulapati was the head of the family. It was a patriarchal society.
Q7: What was the position of women?
Ans: Women held a high position in society. They were educated and participated in religious ceremonies. There was no child marriage or purdah system.
Q8: Who was the Rajan?
Ans: The Rajan was the king or the chief of the tribe (Jana).
Q9: What were the Sabha and Samiti?
Ans: They were two assemblies that advised the king. The Sabha was a council of elders, and the Samiti was a general assembly of the people.
Q10: What was the main occupation of the Early Vedic people?
Ans: Cattle rearing was the main occupation, followed by agriculture.
Q11: Who were the main gods worshipped?
Ans: Indra (God of Rain/Thunder), Agni (God of Fire), Varuna (God of Water), and Surya (Sun God).
Q12: What was the staple food?
Ans: Milk and milk products, barley, fruits, and vegetables.
Q13: What was the 'Bali'?
Ans: Bali was a voluntary contribution made by the people to the king.
Q14: Did the caste system exist?
Ans: The caste system was not rigid. It was based on occupation, not birth.
Q15: What animals were domesticated?
Ans: Cows, horses, sheep, goats, and dogs were domesticated. The cow was considered very sacred (Aghanya).
