Gupta Empire - Questions and Answers

Q1: Who was the founder of the Gupta Empire?
Ans: Sri Gupta was the founder, but Chandragupta I is considered the real founder of the Gupta power.
Q2: Why is the Gupta Age called the 'Golden Age' of India?
Ans: Because of the tremendous progress in art, literature, science, mathematics, and overall prosperity during this period.
Q3: Who was Samudragupta?
Ans: He was the son of Chandragupta I and one of the greatest rulers of the dynasty. He is often called the 'Napoleon of India' for his military conquests.
Q4: What is the Allahabad Pillar Inscription?
Ans: It is an inscription composed by Harisena, the court poet of Samudragupta, praising his achievements (Prayag Prashasti).
Q5: Who assumed the title 'Vikramaditya'?
Ans: Chandragupta II assumed the title of Vikramaditya.
Q6: Who was Kalidasa?
Ans: Kalidasa was a great Sanskrit poet and dramatist in the court of Chandragupta II. He wrote Shakuntala and Meghaduta.
Q7: Who was Aryabhata?
Ans: Aryabhata was a famous mathematician and astronomer. He wrote Aryabhatiya and discovered that the earth rotates on its axis and revolves around the sun. He also gave the concept of zero.
Q8: Who was Fa-Hien?
Ans: Fa-Hien was a Chinese Buddhist pilgrim who visited India during the reign of Chandragupta II.
Q9: What was the main language of the Gupta period?
Ans: Sanskrit was the court language and the language of the learned.
Q10: What were the paintings at Ajanta famous for?
Ans: The Ajanta caves have beautiful frescoes depicting the life of Buddha and Jataka tales.
Q11: What was the Iron Pillar at Mehrauli known for?
Ans: It is known for its rust-resistant quality, showcasing the advanced metallurgical skills of the Gupta period.
Q12: What was the university of Nalanda?
Ans: It was a famous centre of learning (university) that flourished during the Gupta period and later.
Q13: Who wrote the Panchatantra?
Ans: Vishnu Sharma is believed to have written the Panchatantra during this period.
Q14: What religion did the Gupta kings follow?
Ans: They were followers of Vaishnavism (Hinduism) but were tolerant towards other religions like Buddhism and Jainism.
Q15: What led to the decline of the Gupta Empire?
Ans: Invasions by the Huns, weak successors, and the rise of independent regional powers led to the decline.
