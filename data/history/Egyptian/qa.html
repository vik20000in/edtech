Egypt History - Questions and Answers

Q: Who was Herodotus and what did he say about Egypt?

Herodotus was the ancient Greek historian who said that Egypt was 'The Gift of the Nile'[cite: 1].

Q: Who were the Hyksos tribes?

They were tribes that invaded and ruled Egypt for about a hundred years (1,700 BCE - 1,600 BCE)[cite: 2].

Q: Who was Ptolemy?

He was one of the generals of Alexander the Great and the founder of the last dynasty of Egypt[cite: 3].

Q: What was a Pharaoh?

A Pharaoh was an Egyptian King[cite: 4].

Q: Who was Cleopatra?

She was the famous queen of Egypt whose death in 30 BCE marked the end of the Egyptian civilization[cite: 5].

Q: What is the Great Pyramid at Giza?

It is the largest pyramid in Egypt, built by King Khufu (or Cheops), and is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World[cite: 6, 7].

Q: What is a Sphinx?

It is an architectural marvel of ancient Egypt, a mythological figure with the body of a lion and the face of a man[cite: 7, 8].

Q: What is Mummification?

This is the preservation of dead bodies by embalming them[cite: 9]. The preserved bodies are called mummies[cite: 9].

Q: Who was Ra?

He was the sun God of the Egyptian civilization and their most important god[cite: 10].

Q: Who was Osiris?

He was the god of the blessed dead[cite: 11].

Q: What were Nomes?

These were the provinces of the Egyptian civilization[cite: 11].

Q: What was a Nomarch?

He was the official who governed each province (nome)[cite: 12].

Q: What was the Hieroglyphic Script?

It was the writing developed by the Egyptians[cite: 12]. Hieroglyphic means 'sacred writing'[cite: 13].

Q: What is Papyrus?

Papyrus Plant is a water reed from which the ancient Egyptians made paper[cite: 13]. The word 'paper' is derived from papyrus[cite: 14].

Q: How did the Nile river benefit Egypt?

The Nile provided water for irrigation and served as a national highway[cite: 15]. It made the land fertile, allowing crops to be grown year after year[cite: 49, 50]. It also promoted trade and commerce[cite: 51, 52].

Q: What was the staple diet of ancient Egyptians?

The staple diet consisted of wheat and beer[cite: 16].

Q: What was the chief occupation of ancient Egyptians?

The chief occupation was agriculture[cite: 18, 70].

Q: Which Egyptian monument is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World?

The Great Pyramid at Giza, built by King Khufu or Cheops[cite: 40].

Q: What is a mummy?

A preserved dead body is called a Mummy[cite: 41].

Q: How was a body mummified?

A dead body was embalmed with spices and wrapped in fine linen[cite: 42].

Q: Why did the ancient Egyptians preserve the bodies of the dead?

The Egyptians believed in life after death, so they preserved the bodies of the dead[cite: 44].

Q: Who founded the city of Alexandria?

Alexander the Great founded the city of Alexandria[cite: 47].

Q: What materials were Egyptian houses typically made of?

Houses were generally made of sun-baked bricks, though the richest people lived in houses usually made of stone[cite: 55, 57].

Q: Who was the head of the family in ancient Egypt?

The father was the head of the family[cite: 25, 65].

Q: What were the main social classes in ancient Egypt?

The Egyptians were divided into three distinct classes: the upper class (royal family, rich landowners, high priests, officials, doctors), the middle class (merchants, manufacturers, craftspersons, lower clergy), and the lower class (peasants), with slaves at the bottom[cite: 61, 62, 63].

Q: What system was used for trade in ancient Egypt?

The barter system was used[cite: 31, 78]. The mediums of exchange were usually grain, silver, and copper[cite: 78].

Q: What items did the Egyptians export and import?

They exported linen, wheat, glassware, jewelry, and furniture[cite: 76, 107]. They imported gold, ivory, copper, and cedar wood[cite: 77, 107].
