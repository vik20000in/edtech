Egyptian Civilization - Questions and Answers

Q1: On the banks of which river did the Egyptian Civilization develop?
Ans: It developed on the banks of the River Nile.
Q2: Why is Egypt called the 'Gift of the Nile'?
Ans: Because without the Nile, Egypt would have been a desert. The river provided water for irrigation and fertile soil.
Q3: Who was the Pharaoh?
Ans: The king of Egypt was called the Pharaoh. He was considered a god on earth.
Q4: What are Pyramids?
Ans: Pyramids are massive stone structures built as tombs for the Pharaohs.
Q5: What is a Mummy?
Ans: A dead body preserved with chemicals and wrapped in linen cloth is called a mummy.
Q6: What is the Sphinx?
Ans: The Sphinx is a mythological creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human. The Great Sphinx of Giza is a famous statue.
Q7: What was the script of the Egyptians called?
Ans: Hieroglyphics (Sacred Carvings).
Q8: What paper did the Egyptians use?
Ans: They used papyrus, a reed plant that grew along the Nile, to make paper.
Q9: Who was the Sun God?
Ans: Ra (or Re) was the Sun God and the most important deity.
Q10: Who was Osiris?
Ans: Osiris was the God of the Dead and the Afterlife.
Q11: What were the main occupations?
Ans: Agriculture, domestication of animals, art, and craft.
Q12: What contributions did Egyptians make to science?
Ans: They developed a solar calendar of 365 days, made advances in medicine (mummification showed knowledge of anatomy), and mathematics.
Q13: Name a famous female Pharaoh.
Ans: Hatshepsut or Cleopatra.
Q14: How was Egyptian society divided?
Ans: It was divided into three classes: Upper class (Pharaoh, nobles, priests), Middle class (merchants, scribes, artisans), and Lower class (peasants, slaves).
Q15: Why did Egyptians preserve dead bodies?
Ans: They believed in life after death and thought the soul would return to the body, so they preserved it.
