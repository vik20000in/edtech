Urban Local Self-Government - Questions and Answers

Q1: What are the three types of urban local bodies?
Ans: 1. Nagar Panchayat (for transitional areas). 2. Municipal Council or Nagar Palika (for smaller towns). 3. Municipal Corporation or Nagar Nigam (for big cities).
Q2: What is a Municipal Corporation?
Ans: It is the local governing body for large cities with a population of more than 10 lakhs (e.g., Delhi, Mumbai).
Q3: Who is the head of the Municipal Corporation?
Ans: The Mayor (Mahapaur) is the political head.
Q4: Who is the administrative head of the Municipal Corporation?
Ans: The Municipal Commissioner, who is an IAS officer appointed by the government.
Q5: What are Councillors?
Ans: The city is divided into wards, and the people of each ward elect a representative known as a Councillor or Parshad.
Q6: What are the functions of the Municipal Corporation?
Ans: Water supply, garbage disposal, building and maintaining roads, street lighting, running schools and hospitals, and registering births and deaths.
Q7: What is a Nagar Palika?
Ans: It is the local body for smaller towns with a population between 20,000 and 10 lakhs.
Q8: Who is the head of the Nagar Palika?
Ans: The Chairperson or President.
Q9: What is a Nagar Panchayat?
Ans: It is set up for areas that are changing from rural to urban (transitional areas).
Q10: What are the sources of income for these bodies?
Ans: Property tax, water tax, entertainment tax, toll tax, and grants from the state government.
Q11: What is Property Tax?
Ans: It is the tax paid by people who own houses or land in the city. It is a major source of income.
Q12: How are decisions made?
Ans: Councillors form committees (e.g., Water Committee, Garbage Committee) to debate and decide on issues. The Commissioner implements these decisions.
Q13: What is the term of these urban bodies?
Ans: The term is 5 years.
Q14: What happens if the Municipal Corporation does not work properly?
Ans: People can protest, file complaints, or approach the courts. The State Government can also dissolve it.
Q15: What was the plague in Surat (1994)?
Ans: It was a disease outbreak caused by poor sanitation and garbage disposal, which led to the cleaning up of the city. Surat is now one of the cleanest cities.
