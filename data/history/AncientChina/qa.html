Ancient China - Questions and Answers

Q1: Which two major rivers flowed through Ancient China?
Ans: The Huang He (Yellow River) and the Yangtze River.
Q2: Why was the Huang He called the 'Sorrow of China'?
Ans: Because it often flooded, causing great destruction to life and property.
Q3: What was the first known dynasty of China?
Ans: The Shang Dynasty.
Q4: What is the Great Wall of China?
Ans: It is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, built to protect the Chinese states from nomadic invasions.
Q5: Who was Confucius?
Ans: Confucius was a Chinese philosopher and politician whose teachings, called Confucianism, emphasized personal and governmental morality.
Q6: What is Taoism?
Ans: Taoism is a philosophical and religious tradition that emphasizes living in harmony with the Tao (the Way). It was founded by Lao Tzu.
Q7: What was the Silk Road?
Ans: The Silk Road was a network of trade routes connecting the East and West, central to the economic, cultural, political, and religious interactions.
Q8: What were the major inventions of Ancient China?
Ans: Papermaking, printing, gunpowder, and the compass (the Four Great Inventions).
Q9: What material was used for writing before paper?
Ans: Bamboo strips and silk were used for writing before the invention of paper.
Q10: What is the Terracotta Army?
Ans: It is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China.
Q11: What was the main occupation of the people?
Ans: Agriculture was the main occupation. They grew rice, wheat, and millet.
Q12: What is Sericulture?
Ans: The production of silk and the rearing of silkworms for this purpose is called sericulture. The Chinese were the first to discover silk.
Q13: Who was the first Emperor of unified China?
Ans: Qin Shi Huang was the first Emperor of a unified China.
Q14: What is the significance of the dragon in Chinese culture?
Ans: The dragon is a symbol of power, strength, and good luck for people who are worthy of it.
Q15: What was the Mandate of Heaven?
Ans: It was the idea that there could be only one legitimate ruler of China at a time, and that this ruler had the blessing of the gods.
