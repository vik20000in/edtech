Mesopotamian Civilization - Questions and Answers

Q1: What does the word 'Mesopotamia' mean?
Ans: It means 'land between the rivers' in Greek.
Q2: Which two rivers is Mesopotamia associated with?
Ans: The Tigris and Euphrates rivers (in modern-day Iraq).
Q3: What were the main regions of Mesopotamia?
Ans: Sumer (South), Babylonia (Central), and Assyria (North).
Q4: What is a Ziggurat?
Ans: A Ziggurat was a massive temple tower built in honor of the city's patron god. It looked like a stepped pyramid.
Q5: What was the script of Mesopotamia called?
Ans: Cuneiform (wedge-shaped) script.
Q6: Who was Hammurabi?
Ans: Hammurabi was a famous King of Babylon known for his Code of Laws.
Q7: What was Hammurabi's Code based on?
Ans: It was based on the principle of 'an eye for an eye and a tooth for a tooth'.
Q8: What is the Epic of Gilgamesh?
Ans: It is one of the oldest surviving works of literature, an epic poem from Mesopotamia.
Q9: What were the Hanging Gardens of Babylon?
Ans: One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, built by King Nebuchadnezzar II.
Q10: What contributions did Mesopotamians make to mathematics?
Ans: They developed a number system based on 60 (sexagesimal), which gave us the 60-minute hour and 360-degree circle.
Q11: What material did they write on?
Ans: They wrote on wet clay tablets using a stylus made of reed.
Q12: What was the main occupation?
Ans: Agriculture was the main occupation. They also engaged in trade.
Q13: Who were the Sumerians?
Ans: The Sumerians were the first people to settle in Mesopotamia and build cities like Ur and Uruk.
Q14: Did they believe in one god or many gods?
Ans: They were polytheistic, meaning they believed in many gods (e.g., Enlil, Enki).
Q15: What invention helped in transport?
Ans: The invention of the wheel was a major contribution of the Sumerians.
