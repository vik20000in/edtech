Mesopotamian History - Questions and Answers

Q1: What does the name 'Mesopotamia' mean?

Mesopotamia comes from Greek words meaning 'land between the rivers'.

Q2: Which two rivers were most important to Mesopotamian civilization?

The Tigris and Euphrates rivers were the most important.

Q3: Why were these rivers important?

They provided water for drinking and irrigation (watering crops), made the land fertile through flooding, and were used for transportation and trade.

Q4: Who were the Sumerians?

The Sumerians were among the earliest people to settle in southern Mesopotamia (around 4000 BCE) and are credited with developing the first cities and writing system.

Q5: What is a 'city-state'?

A city-state was an independent city and the surrounding farmland it controlled. Examples include Ur and Uruk.

Q6: What were Ziggurats?

Ziggurats were large, stepped pyramid-like temples built in the center of Mesopotamian cities, dedicated to the city's main god or goddess.

Q7: What was the Mesopotamian writing system called?

It was called Cuneiform, which means 'wedge-shaped', because of the marks made by a stylus on clay tablets.

Q8: What did Mesopotamians write on?

They wrote on soft clay tablets using a stylus (a tool often made from a reed).

Q9: Name two important inventions credited to the Mesopotamians (besides writing).

The wheel (first used for pottery, then transport) and the plough (for farming) are two major inventions.

Q10: How did the Mesopotamians contribute to mathematics and time-keeping?

They developed a number system based on 60, which gave us the 60-second minute and 60-minute hour. They also divided the circle into 360 degrees and created the first calendars based on lunar months.

Q11: Who was Hammurabi?

Hammurabi was a famous king of Babylon (around 1792-1750 BCE).

Q12: What is Hammurabi most famous for?

He is most famous for his Code of Laws (the Code of Hammurabi), one of the earliest and most complete written legal codes discovered.
