Later Vedic Period - Questions and Answers

Q1: Which Vedas were composed during the Later Vedic Period?
Ans: Sama Veda, Yajur Veda, and Atharva Veda.
Q2: How did the position of the King change?
Ans: The King became more powerful and his position became hereditary. He performed grand sacrifices like Ashvamedha and Rajasuya.
Q3: What happened to the Sabha and Samiti?
Ans: Their importance declined, and the King's power increased.
Q4: How did the caste system change?
Ans: The caste system became rigid and hereditary. Society was divided into four Varnas: Brahmins, Kshatriyas, Vaishyas, and Shudras.
Q5: What was the position of women?
Ans: The position of women declined. They were denied education and could not participate in political assemblies. Child marriage began to appear.
Q6: What were the main occupations?
Ans: Agriculture became the primary occupation. Use of iron ploughshares improved cultivation. Crafts like weaving, pottery, and metalwork also developed.
Q7: Which gods became important?
Ans: Prajapati (the Creator), Vishnu (the Preserver), and Shiva (the Destroyer) became important, replacing Indra and Agni.
Q8: What are the Upanishads?
Ans: They are philosophical texts that deal with the soul (Atman) and the universal soul (Brahman). They were composed towards the end of the Vedic period.
Q9: What are the Epics?
Ans: The Ramayana and the Mahabharata are the two great epics of this period.
Q10: What was the Ashrama system?
Ans: The life of an upper-caste man was divided into four stages: Brahmacharya (Student), Grihastha (Householder), Vanaprastha (Hermit), and Sannyasa (Renunciate).
Q11: What was the Gurukul system?
Ans: A system of education where students lived with the teacher (Guru) in his house (Ashram) to gain knowledge.
Q12: What is Painted Grey Ware (PGW)?
Ans: It is a distinctive type of pottery found in the Later Vedic sites, made of fine grey clay with painted designs.
Q13: What was the importance of sacrifices?
Ans: Sacrifices (Yajnas) became very complex and expensive. Brahmins became very important for performing these rituals.
Q14: What metal came into wide use?
Ans: Iron (Shyama Ayas) came into wide use for making tools and weapons.
Q15: What were Janapadas?
Ans: Small tribal states (Janas) expanded and became territorial states called Janapadas.
