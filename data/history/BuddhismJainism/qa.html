Buddhism and Jainism - Questions and Answers

Q1: Who was the founder of Buddhism?
Ans: Gautama Buddha (Siddhartha) was the founder of Buddhism.
Q2: Where was Gautama Buddha born?
Ans: He was born in Lumbini (in present-day Nepal).
Q3: What are the Four Noble Truths?
Ans: 1. Life is full of suffering. 2. The cause of suffering is desire. 3. Suffering can end by overcoming desire. 4. The way to end desire is the Eightfold Path.
Q4: What is the Eightfold Path?
Ans: It consists of Right View, Right Resolve, Right Speech, Right Conduct, Right Livelihood, Right Effort, Right Mindfulness, and Right Concentration.
Q5: Who was the founder of Jainism?
Ans: Rishabhanatha was the first Tirthankara, but Mahavira is considered the real founder of Jainism.
Q6: Who was the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism?
Ans: Vardhamana Mahavira.
Q7: What are the Three Jewels (Triratna) of Jainism?
Ans: Right Faith, Right Knowledge, and Right Conduct.
Q8: What is Ahimsa?
Ans: Ahimsa means non-violence. Both Buddhism and Jainism emphasized non-violence towards all living beings.
Q9: In which language did Buddha preach?
Ans: Buddha preached in Pali, the language of the common people.
Q10: In which language did Mahavira preach?
Ans: Mahavira preached in Prakrit.
Q11: What is Nirvana?
Ans: Nirvana is the state of freedom from the cycle of birth and rebirth. It is the ultimate goal in Buddhism.
Q12: What are the two sects of Buddhism?
Ans: Hinayana and Mahayana.
Q13: What are the two sects of Jainism?
Ans: Digambara (sky-clad) and Svetambara (white-clad).
Q14: Where did Buddha deliver his first sermon?
Ans: At Sarnath, near Varanasi.
Q15: What is the Tripitaka?
Ans: The Tripitaka (Three Baskets) are the sacred texts of Buddhism: Vinaya Pitaka, Sutta Pitaka, and Abhidhamma Pitaka.
