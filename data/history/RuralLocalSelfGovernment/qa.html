Rural Local Self-Government - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is Local Self-Government?
Ans: It is the government elected by the people at the local level (village or town) to manage local affairs.
Q2: What is the Panchayati Raj System?
Ans: It is a three-tier system of rural local self-government in India.
Q3: What are the three levels of Panchayati Raj?
Ans: 1. Gram Panchayat (Village level). 2. Block Samiti or Panchayat Samiti (Block level). 3. Zila Parishad (District level).
Q4: What is the Gram Sabha?
Ans: It is the general assembly of all adults (18+ years) living in the village. It elects the Gram Panchayat.
Q5: Who is the head of the Gram Panchayat?
Ans: The Sarpanch or Pradhan.
Q6: What are the functions of the Gram Panchayat?
Ans: Construction and maintenance of roads, drainage, water sources, street lighting, and looking after health and sanitation.
Q7: What is the Nyaya Panchayat?
Ans: It is the village court that settles minor disputes and provides cheap and speedy justice.
Q8: Who is the Panchayat Secretary?
Ans: He is a government official appointed to maintain records and accounts of the Panchayat.
Q9: What is the Block Samiti?
Ans: It is the link between the Gram Panchayat and the Zila Parishad. It supervises the working of Panchayats in a block.
Q10: Who is the head of the Block Samiti?
Ans: The Block Pramukh or Chairman.
Q11: What is the Zila Parishad?
Ans: It is the highest body of the Panchayati Raj system at the district level.
Q12: Who is the head of the Zila Parishad?
Ans: The Zila Parishad Chairperson.
Q13: Are there reserved seats in Panchayats?
Ans: Yes, seats are reserved for Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and women (at least one-third).
Q14: What is the term of the Panchayat?
Ans: The term is 5 years.
Q15: Why is Panchayati Raj important?
Ans: It strengthens democracy at the grassroots level and allows people to participate in their own development.
