Mauryan Empire - Questions and Answers

Q1: Who founded the Mauryan Empire?
Ans: Chandragupta Maurya founded the Mauryan Empire in 321 BCE.
Q2: Who helped Chandragupta Maurya defeat the Nandas?
Ans: Chanakya (also known as Kautilya or Vishnugupta).
Q3: What is the Arthashastra?
Ans: It is a book on statecraft, politics, and administration written by Chanakya.
Q4: Who was Megasthenes?
Ans: He was a Greek ambassador sent by Seleucus Nicator to the court of Chandragupta Maurya. He wrote the book 'Indica'.
Q5: Who was Ashoka?
Ans: Ashoka was the grandson of Chandragupta Maurya and one of the greatest rulers of India.
Q6: Which war changed Ashoka's life?
Ans: The Kalinga War (261 BCE). The bloodshed and suffering moved him to give up war and embrace Buddhism.
Q7: What is Ashoka's Dhamma?
Ans: It was a code of conduct inspired by Buddhism, emphasizing non-violence, truthfulness, respect for elders, and kindness to all beings.
Q8: How did Ashoka spread Dhamma?
Ans: He appointed officials called Dhamma Mahamattas, inscribed his messages on rocks and pillars, and sent messengers to other countries.
Q9: What is the Sanchi Stupa?
Ans: It is a famous Buddhist monument built by Ashoka.
Q10: What is our National Emblem?
Ans: The Lion Capital of Ashoka from Sarnath is our National Emblem.
Q11: How was the Mauryan administration organized?
Ans: It was highly centralized. The empire was divided into provinces, districts, and villages. Pataliputra was directly administered by the Emperor.
Q12: What happened to the Mauryan Empire after Ashoka?
Ans: The empire declined due to weak successors and was finally ended when the last ruler Brihadratha was killed by Pushyamitra Shunga.
Q13: What script were Ashoka's edicts written in?
Ans: Most were written in Brahmi script.
Q14: Who was Bindusara?
Ans: Bindusara was the son of Chandragupta Maurya and the father of Ashoka.
Q15: What did Chandragupta Maurya do in his last days?
Ans: He embraced Jainism, abdicated the throne, and went to Shravanabelagola (Karnataka) where he fasted to death.
