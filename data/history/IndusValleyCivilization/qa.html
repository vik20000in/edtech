Indus Valley Civilization - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is the other name for the Indus Valley Civilization?
Ans: Harappan Civilization.
Q2: Which were the two most important cities?
Ans: Harappa and Mohenjo-daro.
Q3: What was the most striking feature of the Indus cities?
Ans: Town planning. The cities were well-planned with a grid system of roads and an excellent drainage system.
Q4: What was the Great Bath?
Ans: It was a large rectangular tank in Mohenjo-daro, likely used for religious bathing.
Q5: What was the Citadel?
Ans: The raised part of the city where important buildings like the Great Bath and Granaries were located. The common people lived in the Lower Town.
Q6: What was the main occupation of the people?
Ans: Agriculture. They grew wheat, barley, peas, and cotton.
Q7: Did they know the use of iron?
Ans: No, they did not know the use of iron. They used copper and bronze.
Q8: What were the seals made of?
Ans: Seals were made of steatite (soft stone) and terracotta. They had animal figures and a script carved on them.
Q9: Who was the Mother Goddess?
Ans: A female deity worshipped by the people, representing fertility.
Q10: What is the Dancing Girl?
Ans: A famous bronze statue found at Mohenjo-daro.
Q11: Which animal is most frequently depicted on seals?
Ans: The Unicorn (a mythical animal) and the Humped Bull.
Q12: What was the script of the Indus people?
Ans: It was a pictographic script which has not yet been deciphered.
Q13: What was Lothal famous for?
Ans: Lothal was a famous port city with a dockyard.
Q14: Why did the civilization decline?
Ans: The exact reason is unknown, but theories include floods, earthquakes, change in the course of the river Indus, or Aryan invasion.
Q15: What toys did the children play with?
Ans: Terracotta toys like carts, ploughs, whistles, and animals with movable heads.
