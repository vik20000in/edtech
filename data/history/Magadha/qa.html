Magadha - Questions and Answers

Q1: What were Mahajanapadas?
Ans: By the 6th century BCE, some Janapadas became larger and more powerful than others. These were called Mahajanapadas. There were 16 Mahajanapadas.
Q2: Which was the most powerful Mahajanapada?
Ans: Magadha emerged as the most powerful Mahajanapada.
Q3: Why did Magadha become powerful?
Ans: 1. Fertile land (Ganga basin). 2. Iron mines (for weapons). 3. Forests (elephants for army). 4. Strategic location of capitals.
Q4: Who were the two powerful rulers of Magadha?
Ans: Bimbisara and Ajatashatru.
Q5: What was the capital of Magadha?
Ans: Rajagriha was the first capital, later shifted to Pataliputra (modern Patna).
Q6: Who was the founder of the Nanda Dynasty?
Ans: Mahapadma Nanda.
Q7: Who was the last ruler of the Nanda Dynasty?
Ans: Dhana Nanda. He was defeated by Chandragupta Maurya.
Q8: Who was Alexander?
Ans: Alexander was the ruler of Macedonia (Greece) who invaded India in 326 BCE.
Q9: Why did Alexander not advance further into India?
Ans: His soldiers were tired, homesick, and scared of the mighty army of the Nandas of Magadha, so they refused to go further.
Q10: What is a Monarchy?
Ans: A form of government where the king has absolute power and the position is hereditary (e.g., Magadha).
Q11: What is a Gana-Sangha (Republic)?
Ans: A form of government where power is shared by a group of people, not a single king (e.g., Vajji).
Q12: What was the capital of Vajji?
Ans: Vaishali.
Q13: How did Bimbisara expand his empire?
Ans: Through matrimonial alliances and conquests.
Q14: What weapon did Ajatashatru use?
Ans: He used two new weapons: Mahashilakantaka (a large catapult) and Rathamusala (a chariot with a mace).
Q15: What was the importance of Pataliputra?
Ans: It was a water fort (Jaladurga) situated at the confluence of rivers, making it safe and a centre of trade.
