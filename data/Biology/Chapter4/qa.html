Digestive System - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is digestion?
Ans: Digestion is the process of breaking down complex food substances into simpler, soluble forms that can be absorbed by the body.
Q2: Name the organs of the alimentary canal.
Ans: Mouth, Oesophagus (Food pipe), Stomach, Small Intestine, Large Intestine, Rectum, and Anus.
Q3: What is the role of saliva in digestion?
Ans: Saliva moistens the food and contains an enzyme called salivary amylase that breaks down starch into sugar.
Q4: What happens to food in the stomach?
Ans: In the stomach, food is churned and mixed with gastric juices containing hydrochloric acid and enzymes (pepsin) that digest proteins.
Q5: What is the function of the small intestine?
Ans: The small intestine is the site of complete digestion and absorption of nutrients into the blood.
Q6: What is the role of the liver?
Ans: The liver produces bile juice, which helps in the digestion of fats (emulsification).
Q7: What is the function of the pancreas?
Ans: The pancreas secretes pancreatic juice containing enzymes that digest carbohydrates, proteins, and fats.
Q8: What are villi?
Ans: Villi are finger-like projections on the inner wall of the small intestine that increase the surface area for absorption of digested food.
Q9: What is the function of the large intestine?
Ans: The large intestine absorbs water and salts from the undigested food and stores the waste until egestion.
Q10: What is peristalsis?
Ans: The wave-like muscular contractions of the alimentary canal that push the food forward are called peristalsis.
Q11: What is assimilation?
Ans: Assimilation is the process by which absorbed nutrients are utilized by the body cells for energy, growth, and repair.
Q12: What is egestion?
Ans: The process of removing undigested waste material (faeces) from the body through the anus is called egestion.
Q13: Name the acid produced in the stomach.
Ans: Hydrochloric acid (HCl).
Q14: Where is bile stored?
Ans: Bile is stored in the gall bladder.
Q15: What are enzymes?
Ans: Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in the body, such as digestion.
