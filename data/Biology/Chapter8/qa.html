Adaptation - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is adaptation?
Ans: The presence of specific features or habits which enable a plant or an animal to live in a particular habitat is called adaptation.
Q2: What is a habitat?
Ans: The surroundings where an organism lives is called its habitat. It provides food, water, air, shelter, and other needs.
Q3: What are terrestrial animals?
Ans: Animals that live on land are called terrestrial animals. Examples: Lion, Cow, Human.
Q4: What are aquatic animals?
Ans: Animals that live in water are called aquatic animals. Examples: Fish, Whale, Octopus.
Q5: What are amphibians?
Ans: Animals that can live both on land and in water are called amphibians. Examples: Frog, Toad.
Q6: How is a camel adapted to the desert?
Ans: A camel has a hump to store fat, long legs to keep the body away from hot sand, broad feet to walk on sand, and long eyelashes to protect from sandstorms.
Q7: How are fish adapted to water?
Ans: Fish have a streamlined body to swim easily, gills for breathing, and fins/tail for movement and balance.
Q8: What are arboreal animals?
Ans: Animals that spend most of their time on trees are called arboreal animals. Example: Monkey.
Q9: What are aerial animals?
Ans: Animals that can fly and spend a lot of time in the air are called aerial animals. Example: Birds, Bats.
Q10: How are birds adapted for flying?
Ans: Birds have hollow bones (lightweight), wings with feathers, a streamlined body, and strong flight muscles.
Q11: What is hibernation?
Ans: The deep winter sleep of some animals (like bears, frogs) to survive extreme cold and lack of food is called hibernation.
Q12: What is camouflage?
Ans: The ability of an animal to blend in with its surroundings to hide from predators or prey is called camouflage. Example: Chameleon.
Q13: How do cactus plants survive in deserts?
Ans: Cactus plants have leaves modified into spines to reduce water loss, a thick green stem to store water and perform photosynthesis, and deep roots.
Q14: What are hydrophytes?
Ans: Plants that live in water are called hydrophytes. Example: Lotus, Hydrilla.
Q15: What are xerophytes?
Ans: Plants that live in dry conditions like deserts are called xerophytes. Example: Cactus, Aloe Vera.
