Respiratory System - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is respiration?
Ans: Respiration is the process of taking in oxygen, using it to break down food to release energy, and eliminating carbon dioxide.
Q2: Differentiate between breathing and respiration.
Ans: Breathing is the physical process of inhaling and exhaling air. Respiration is the chemical process of releasing energy from food.
Q3: Name the organs of the human respiratory system.
Ans: Nostrils, Nasal cavity, Pharynx, Larynx, Trachea (Windpipe), Bronchi, and Lungs.
Q4: What is the function of the trachea?
Ans: The trachea or windpipe carries air from the throat to the lungs. It is supported by C-shaped cartilage rings to prevent collapse.
Q5: What are alveoli?
Ans: Alveoli are tiny air sacs in the lungs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place.
Q6: What is the diaphragm?
Ans: The diaphragm is a muscular sheet below the lungs that helps in breathing by moving up and down to change the chest cavity volume.
Q7: What happens during inhalation?
Ans: During inhalation, the diaphragm moves down, the ribs move up and out, the chest cavity expands, and air rushes into the lungs.
Q8: What happens during exhalation?
Ans: During exhalation, the diaphragm moves up, the ribs move down and in, the chest cavity contracts, and air is pushed out of the lungs.
Q9: What is aerobic respiration?
Ans: Respiration that takes place in the presence of oxygen is called aerobic respiration. It produces carbon dioxide, water, and energy.
Q10: What is anaerobic respiration?
Ans: Respiration that takes place in the absence of oxygen is called anaerobic respiration. It produces less energy.
Q11: Why do we sneeze?
Ans: We sneeze to expel foreign particles like dust or pollen that irritate the nasal lining.
Q12: What is the epiglottis?
Ans: The epiglottis is a flap-like structure that covers the windpipe during swallowing to prevent food from entering the lungs.
Q13: How do fish breathe?
Ans: Fish breathe through gills, which extract dissolved oxygen from water.
Q14: How do earthworms breathe?
Ans: Earthworms breathe through their moist skin.
Q15: What is the breathing rate?
Ans: The number of times a person breathes in a minute is called the breathing rate. Normal rate is 15-18 times per minute.
