The Leaf - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a leaf?
Ans: A leaf is a flattened, green, lateral outgrowth of the stem of a vascular plant. It is the primary site of photosynthesis.
Q2: What are the main parts of a leaf?
Ans: The main parts of a leaf are the leaf base, petiole (stalk), and lamina (leaf blade).
Q3: What is photosynthesis?
Ans: Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water in the presence of chlorophyll.
Q4: What is chlorophyll?
Ans: Chlorophyll is the green pigment present in leaves that traps sunlight energy for photosynthesis.
Q5: What are stomata?
Ans: Stomata are tiny pores present on the surface of leaves (mostly on the underside) that help in the exchange of gases and transpiration.
Q6: What is transpiration?
Ans: The loss of excess water in the form of water vapour from the aerial parts of the plant (mainly leaves) is called transpiration.
Q7: Differentiate between simple and compound leaves.
Ans: A simple leaf has a single undivided blade (e.g., Mango). A compound leaf has the blade divided into smaller leaflets (e.g., Rose).
Q8: What is venation? Types?
Ans: The arrangement of veins and veinlets in the leaf lamina is called venation. Two types: Reticulate (net-like) and Parallel.
Q9: What is the function of veins in a leaf?
Ans: Veins transport water, minerals, and food to and from the leaf. They also provide structural support to the leaf blade.
Q10: What is a tendril?
Ans: A tendril is a modified leaf or stem used by climbing plants for support and attachment. Example: Pea plant leaf tendrils.
Q11: What are spines?
Ans: Spines are modified leaves found in desert plants (like Cactus) to reduce water loss by transpiration and protect the plant from grazing animals.
Q12: What is vegetative propagation by leaves?
Ans: Some plants like Bryophyllum can reproduce through their leaves. New plants grow from the buds present on the leaf margins.
Q13: Why are leaves called the 'Kitchen of the Plant'?
Ans: Leaves are called the kitchen of the plant because they prepare food for the entire plant through photosynthesis.
Q14: What is the petiole?
Ans: The stalk that attaches the leaf blade to the stem is called the petiole.
Q15: What is the midrib?
Ans: The thick vein running through the centre of the leaf is called the midrib.
