The Cell - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a cell?
Ans: A cell is the structural and functional unit of life. All living organisms are made up of cells.
Q2: Who discovered the cell?
Ans: Robert Hooke discovered the cell in 1665.
Q3: What are the three main parts of a cell?
Ans: The three main parts are the Cell Membrane, Cytoplasm, and Nucleus.
Q4: What is the function of the nucleus?
Ans: The nucleus controls all the activities of the cell. It contains genetic material (DNA).
Q5: What is the cell membrane?
Ans: The cell membrane is the outer protective covering of the cell. It controls the entry and exit of substances.
Q6: Differentiate between plant and animal cells.
Ans: Plant cells have a cell wall and chloroplasts, which are absent in animal cells. Animal cells have centrioles, which are absent in plant cells. Plant cells usually have a large central vacuole.
Q7: What is the function of mitochondria?
Ans: Mitochondria are called the 'Powerhouse of the cell' because they produce energy through respiration.
Q8: What are chloroplasts?
Ans: Chloroplasts are green plastids found in plant cells that contain chlorophyll and help in photosynthesis.
Q9: What is the function of the cell wall?
Ans: The cell wall provides rigidity, shape, and protection to the plant cell. It is made of cellulose.
Q10: What is cytoplasm?
Ans: Cytoplasm is the jelly-like substance present between the cell membrane and the nucleus. It contains cell organelles.
Q11: What are unicellular and multicellular organisms?
Ans: Unicellular organisms are made of a single cell (e.g., Amoeba). Multicellular organisms are made of many cells (e.g., Humans).
Q12: What are chromosomes?
Ans: Chromosomes are thread-like structures present in the nucleus that carry genes and transfer characteristics from parents to offspring.
Q13: What is a tissue?
Ans: A group of similar cells performing a specific function is called a tissue.
Q14: What are vacuoles?
Ans: Vacuoles are sac-like structures used for storing food, water, and waste. They are large in plant cells and small or absent in animal cells.
Q15: Why is the nucleus called the brain of the cell?
Ans: Because it regulates and coordinates all the vital activities of the cell.
