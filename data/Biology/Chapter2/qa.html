The Flower - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a flower?
Ans: A flower is the reproductive part of a plant. It contains the reproductive organs.
Q2: What are the four whorls of a flower?
Ans: The four whorls are Calyx (Sepals), Corolla (Petals), Androecium (Stamens), and Gynoecium (Pistil/Carpel).
Q3: What is the function of sepals?
Ans: Sepals (green outer part) protect the flower in the bud stage.
Q4: What is the function of petals?
Ans: Petals are usually colourful and scented to attract insects for pollination.
Q5: What is the male reproductive part of a flower?
Ans: The Androecium or Stamen is the male reproductive part. It consists of an anther and a filament.
Q6: What is the female reproductive part of a flower?
Ans: The Gynoecium or Pistil is the female reproductive part. It consists of stigma, style, and ovary.
Q7: What is pollination?
Ans: The transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of a flower is called pollination.
Q8: Differentiate between self-pollination and cross-pollination.
Ans: Self-pollination occurs within the same flower or between flowers of the same plant. Cross-pollination occurs between flowers of different plants of the same species.
Q9: What is fertilization?
Ans: The fusion of the male gamete with the female gamete (egg) inside the ovary is called fertilization.
Q10: What happens to the ovary after fertilization?
Ans: After fertilization, the ovary ripens to form the fruit, and the ovules develop into seeds.
Q11: What are bisexual flowers?
Ans: Flowers that contain both male (stamen) and female (pistil) reproductive parts are called bisexual or complete flowers. Example: Hibiscus, Rose.
Q12: What are unisexual flowers?
Ans: Flowers that contain either only male or only female reproductive parts are called unisexual or incomplete flowers. Example: Papaya, Cucumber.
Q13: What are the agents of pollination?
Ans: Wind, water, insects, birds, and animals are agents of pollination.
Q14: What is the function of the anther?
Ans: The anther produces pollen grains which contain the male gametes.
Q15: What is nectar?
Ans: Nectar is a sweet liquid produced by flowers to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
