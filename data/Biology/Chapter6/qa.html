Circulatory System - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is the circulatory system?
Ans: The organ system responsible for transporting blood, nutrients, oxygen, and waste products throughout the body is called the circulatory system.
Q2: What are the main components of the circulatory system?
Ans: Heart, Blood, and Blood Vessels.
Q3: What is the function of the heart?
Ans: The heart acts as a pump to circulate blood throughout the body.
Q4: Name the four chambers of the human heart.
Ans: Right Atrium, Right Ventricle, Left Atrium, and Left Ventricle.
Q5: What are the three types of blood vessels?
Ans: Arteries, Veins, and Capillaries.
Q6: Differentiate between arteries and veins.
Ans: Arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart (except pulmonary artery). Veins carry deoxygenated blood towards the heart (except pulmonary vein). Arteries have thick walls; veins have thin walls and valves.
Q7: What are capillaries?
Ans: Capillaries are extremely thin blood vessels that connect arteries and veins. Exchange of materials occurs through their walls.
Q8: What are the components of blood?
Ans: Plasma, Red Blood Cells (RBCs), White Blood Cells (WBCs), and Platelets.
Q9: What is the function of RBCs?
Ans: RBCs contain haemoglobin which carries oxygen from the lungs to all body parts.
Q10: What is the function of WBCs?
Ans: WBCs fight against germs and protect the body from diseases.
Q11: What is the function of platelets?
Ans: Platelets help in the clotting of blood to stop bleeding.
Q12: What is the pulse?
Ans: The rhythmic throbbing of arteries caused by the pumping action of the heart is called the pulse.
Q13: What is the normal heart rate of an adult?
Ans: The normal heart rate is 72 beats per minute.
Q14: What is the septum?
Ans: The muscular wall that separates the left and right sides of the heart is called the septum. It prevents mixing of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.
Q15: What is the stethoscope used for?
Ans: A stethoscope is a medical instrument used by doctors to listen to the heartbeats.
