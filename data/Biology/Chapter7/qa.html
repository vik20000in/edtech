Health and Hygiene - Questions and Answers

Q1: Define health.
Ans: Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being, not merely the absence of disease.
Q2: What is a balanced diet?
Ans: A diet that contains all the essential nutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals) in the right proportions is called a balanced diet.
Q3: What are communicable diseases?
Ans: Diseases that can spread from an infected person to a healthy person are called communicable or infectious diseases. Examples: Flu, Tuberculosis.
Q4: What are non-communicable diseases?
Ans: Diseases that do not spread from one person to another are called non-communicable diseases. Examples: Diabetes, Cancer.
Q5: What are deficiency diseases?
Ans: Diseases caused by the lack of specific nutrients in the diet over a long period are called deficiency diseases. Example: Scurvy (Vitamin C deficiency).
Q6: Name the disease caused by the deficiency of Iron.
Ans: Anaemia.
Q7: Name the disease caused by the deficiency of Iodine.
Ans: Goitre.
Q8: What is hygiene?
Ans: Hygiene refers to practices that help maintain health and prevent the spread of diseases, such as cleanliness.
Q9: How does malaria spread?
Ans: Malaria is spread by the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.
Q10: What is vaccination?
Ans: Vaccination is the administration of a vaccine to stimulate the immune system to develop protection against a specific disease.
Q11: What are vectors?
Ans: Organisms that carry disease-causing germs from an infected person to a healthy person are called vectors. Examples: Mosquitoes, Houseflies.
Q12: Why should we cover food?
Ans: To prevent contamination by dust and flies, which can carry germs and cause diseases.
Q13: What is first aid?
Ans: The immediate help given to an injured or sick person before medical help arrives is called first aid.
Q14: Name a disease caused by bacteria.
Ans: Typhoid, Cholera, Tuberculosis.
Q15: Name a disease caused by a virus.
Ans: Common cold, Influenza, Polio, COVID-19.
