Fundamentals for Algebra - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a variable?
Ans: A variable is a symbol (usually a letter like x, y, z) that represents an unknown value which can change.
Q2: What is a constant?
Ans: A constant is a symbol having a fixed numerical value (e.g., 5, -2, 100).
Q3: What is an algebraic expression?
Ans: A combination of constants and variables connected by fundamental operations (+, -, x, รท) is called an algebraic expression (e.g., 2x + 3).
Q4: What are terms of an expression?
Ans: The parts of an expression separated by + or - signs are called terms. In 2x + 3y, the terms are 2x and 3y.
Q5: What is a coefficient?
Ans: The numerical factor of a term is called its numerical coefficient. In 5xy, 5 is the coefficient.
Q6: Differentiate between like and unlike terms.
Ans: Terms having the same algebraic factors are like terms (e.g., 2x, 5x). Terms having different algebraic factors are unlike terms (e.g., 2x, 3y).
Q7: What is a monomial?
Ans: An expression with only one term is called a monomial (e.g., 4x).
Q8: What is a binomial?
Ans: An expression with two unlike terms is called a binomial (e.g., x + y).
Q9: What is a trinomial?
Ans: An expression with three unlike terms is called a trinomial (e.g., x + y + z).
Q10: Add: 3x and 5x.
Ans: 8x.
Q11: Subtract: 2y from 7y.
Ans: 7y - 2y = 5y.
Q12: Simplify: 2(x + 3)
Ans: 2x + 6.
Q13: What is the value of 2x + 5 if x = 3?
Ans: 2(3) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11.
Q14: Write an expression for: '5 added to y'.
Ans: y + 5.
Q15: Write an expression for: '3 times x subtracted from 10'.
Ans: 10 - 3x.
