Matter - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is matter?
Ans: Anything that has mass and occupies space is called matter.
Q2: What are the three states of matter?
Ans: Solid, Liquid, and Gas.
Q3: What is an atom?
Ans: An atom is the smallest particle of an element that can exist and retains the properties of that element.
Q4: What is a molecule?
Ans: A molecule is the smallest particle of a substance (element or compound) that can exist independently and retains the properties of that substance.
Q5: Define intermolecular force.
Ans: The force of attraction between the molecules of a substance is called intermolecular force.
Q6: Define intermolecular space.
Ans: The space between the molecules of a substance is called intermolecular space.
Q7: Why do solids have a fixed shape and volume?
Ans: Because the molecules in solids are very tightly packed, have strong intermolecular forces, and very little intermolecular space.
Q8: Why do liquids flow?
Ans: In liquids, the molecules are less tightly packed than in solids and can move around each other, allowing liquids to flow.
Q9: Why do gases fill the entire container?
Ans: In gases, the intermolecular force is negligible, and molecules move freely in all directions, filling the entire available space.
Q10: What is diffusion?
Ans: The intermixing of particles of two different substances on their own is called diffusion.
Q11: What is Brownian motion?
Ans: The zig-zag movement of small particles suspended in a liquid or gas is called Brownian motion.
Q12: Can matter change its state?
Ans: Yes, matter can change its state on heating or cooling (e.g., Ice to Water to Steam).
Q13: What is melting point?
Ans: The fixed temperature at which a solid changes into a liquid is called its melting point.
Q14: What is boiling point?
Ans: The fixed temperature at which a liquid starts boiling and changes into gas is called its boiling point.
Q15: What is sublimation?
Ans: The process in which a solid directly changes into gas without becoming liquid on heating is called sublimation (e.g., Camphor, Naphthalene).
