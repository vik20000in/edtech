Physical Quantities and Measurements - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a physical quantity?
Ans: A quantity that can be measured is called a physical quantity. Examples include length, mass, time, and temperature.
Q2: What is the SI unit of length?
Ans: The SI unit of length is the metre (m).
Q3: Define mass. What is its SI unit?
Ans: Mass is the quantity of matter contained in a body. Its SI unit is the kilogram (kg).
Q4: How do we measure the volume of an irregular solid?
Ans: The volume of an irregular solid can be measured using the displacement method with a measuring cylinder and water.
Q5: What is the difference between mass and weight?
Ans: Mass is the amount of matter in a body and remains constant everywhere. Weight is the force with which the earth attracts a body and varies from place to place.
Q6: What is the SI unit of time?
Ans: The SI unit of time is the second (s).
Q7: What is temperature? Name the device used to measure it.
Ans: Temperature is the degree of hotness or coldness of a body. It is measured using a thermometer.
Q8: What is the SI unit of temperature?
Ans: The SI unit of temperature is Kelvin (K). However, Degree Celsius (°C) and Degree Fahrenheit (°F) are also commonly used.
Q9: Define area. What is its SI unit?
Ans: The amount of surface enclosed by a closed figure is called its area. Its SI unit is square metre (m²).
Q10: How many centimetres are there in 1 metre?
Ans: There are 100 centimetres in 1 metre.
Q11: What is parallax error?
Ans: Parallax error is the error in measurement caused by looking at the scale from an angle instead of directly from the front.
Q12: What is the use of a beam balance?
Ans: A beam balance is used to measure the mass of an object by comparing it with standard masses.
Q13: Convert 5 kg into grams.
Ans: 1 kg = 1000 g. Therefore, 5 kg = 5 × 1000 = 5000 g.
Q14: What is the clinical thermometer used for?
Ans: A clinical thermometer is used to measure the temperature of the human body. Its range is usually from 35°C to 42°C.
Q15: What is the normal temperature of the human body?
Ans: The normal temperature of the human body is 37°C or 98.6°F.
