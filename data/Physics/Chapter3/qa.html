Force - Questions and Answers

Q1: Define force.
Ans: Force is a push or pull acting on an object which changes or tends to change the state of rest or of motion of the object.
Q2: What is the SI unit of force?
Ans: The SI unit of force is the Newton (N).
Q3: What are the effects of force?
Ans: Force can move a stationary object, stop a moving object, change the speed or direction of a moving object, and change the shape or size of an object.
Q4: Differentiate between contact and non-contact forces.
Ans: Contact forces act only when objects are in physical contact (e.g., friction, muscular force). Non-contact forces act from a distance without physical contact (e.g., gravitational force, magnetic force).
Q5: What is friction?
Ans: Friction is a force that opposes the relative motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts in the direction opposite to the motion.
Q6: What is gravitational force?
Ans: The force of attraction with which the earth pulls everything towards its centre is called gravitational force or gravity.
Q7: What is electrostatic force?
Ans: The force exerted by a charged body on another charged or uncharged body is called electrostatic force.
Q8: What is magnetic force?
Ans: The force exerted by a magnet on magnetic materials (like iron) or other magnets is called magnetic force.
Q9: Why is friction called a necessary evil?
Ans: Friction is necessary because it helps us walk, write, and stop vehicles. It is an evil because it causes wear and tear of machine parts and wastes energy as heat.
Q10: How can friction be reduced?
Ans: Friction can be reduced by using lubricants (oil, grease), using ball bearings, polishing surfaces, and streamlining the shape of objects.
Q11: How can friction be increased?
Ans: Friction can be increased by making surfaces rough, such as the treads on tyres or the soles of shoes.
Q12: What is muscular force?
Ans: The force exerted by the muscles of our body is called muscular force. It is a contact force.
Q13: Define 1 Newton.
Ans: 1 Newton is the force required to lift a mass of approximately 100g (0.1 kg) vertically upwards.
Q14: What is a spring balance used for?
Ans: A spring balance is a device used to measure the force acting on an object, often used to measure weight.
Q15: Does force always produce motion?
Ans: No, force does not always produce motion. For example, pushing a heavy wall applies force but does not move the wall.
