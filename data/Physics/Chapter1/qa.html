Matter - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is matter?
Ans: Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. It is made up of tiny particles called atoms and molecules.
Q2: What are the three states of matter?
Ans: The three states of matter are solid, liquid, and gas.
Q3: Define intermolecular force.
Ans: The force of attraction between the molecules of a substance is called intermolecular force. It is strongest in solids and weakest in gases.
Q4: What is intermolecular space?
Ans: The space between the molecules of a substance is called intermolecular space. It is minimum in solids and maximum in gases.
Q5: Differentiate between solids and liquids based on shape and volume.
Ans: Solids have a fixed shape and a fixed volume. Liquids have a fixed volume but no fixed shape; they take the shape of the container.
Q6: Why do gases not have a fixed shape or volume?
Ans: In gases, the intermolecular force is negligible, and the intermolecular space is very large. The molecules are free to move in all directions, so they fill the entire space available to them.
Q7: What is the kinetic theory of matter?
Ans: The kinetic theory of matter states that matter is made up of tiny particles that are in constant motion. The energy of motion is called kinetic energy, which increases with temperature.
Q8: Define cohesion and adhesion.
Ans: Cohesion is the force of attraction between molecules of the same substance. Adhesion is the force of attraction between molecules of different substances.
Q9: Why can a liquid flow?
Ans: Liquids can flow because their molecules are not as tightly packed as in solids and can slide over one another.
Q10: What happens to the particles of a solid when it is heated?
Ans: When a solid is heated, its particles gain kinetic energy and vibrate more vigorously. Eventually, they overcome the intermolecular forces and the solid melts into a liquid.
Q11: Why are solids incompressible?
Ans: Solids are incompressible because their particles are closely packed with very little intermolecular space between them.
Q12: Give two examples of fluids.
Ans: Liquids (like water, oil) and gases (like air, oxygen) are fluids because they can flow.
Q13: Arrange solids, liquids, and gases in increasing order of intermolecular force.
Ans: Gas < Liquid < Solid.
Q14: Arrange solids, liquids, and gases in increasing order of intermolecular space.
Ans: Solid < Liquid < Gas.
Q15: What is Brownian motion?
Ans: The zig-zag, random motion of particles suspended in a fluid (liquid or gas) is called Brownian motion.
