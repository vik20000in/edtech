English Language - Articles Q&A

Short Answer Questions

ShortQ1: What is the central idea of Articles?
Ans: The central idea of Articles is connected with a, an and the used before nouns. It helps students understand characters, events, theme and message.
ShortQ2: Name one important skill developed through Articles.
Ans: It develops careful reading, interpretation, vocabulary and the ability to support answers with points from the text.
ShortQ3: Why are characters important in Articles?
Ans: Characters carry the action and reveal the theme through their words, choices and behaviour.
ShortQ4: How should a literature answer be supported?
Ans: A literature answer should be supported with relevant events, character traits, theme and clear explanation in the student’s own words.
ShortQ5: What should be revised in Articles?
Ans: Revise the summary, theme, difficult words, important characters, poetic devices or story elements, and likely short questions.

Long Answer Questions

LongQ6: Explain the theme of Articles.
Ans: Articles presents ideas related to a, an and the used before nouns. The theme can be understood by studying the title, characters, important events and message. A good answer should explain what happens, why it matters and what lesson or reflection the reader receives.
LongQ7: Write a character or content-based answer on Articles.
Ans: While writing about Articles, mention the important character or content point first. Then describe actions, qualities, conflicts and changes. Support the answer with relevant incidents and conclude by linking the character or content to the main theme.
LongQ8: How can Articles be prepared for examination?
Ans: To prepare Articles, read the text carefully, learn the summary, note important lines and understand the theme. Practise short answers for facts and long answers for explanation, character study and message.
LongQ9: Write the message of Articles.
Ans: The message of Articles is best understood through its events, images and characters. It encourages students to think about values, choices and consequences. The answer should be written clearly without copying long passages from the text.
LongQ10: Prepare a short critical note on Articles.
Ans: A critical note on Articles should include the type of text, central idea, important features and personal response. It should be analytical, clear and supported by examples from the lesson or poem.
