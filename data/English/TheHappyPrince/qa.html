The Happy Prince - Questions and Answers

Q1: Who was the Happy Prince?
Ans: The Happy Prince was a beautiful statue covered with gold leaves, having sapphires for eyes and a ruby in his sword-hilt.
Q2: Why was the Prince crying?
Ans: The Prince was crying because he could see all the ugliness and misery of his city from his high pedestal.
Q3: Who became the Prince's messenger?
Ans: A little Swallow, who was left behind by his friends, became the Prince's messenger.
Q4: Where was the Swallow going?
Ans: The Swallow was going to Egypt to join his friends for the winter.
Q5: To whom did the Prince send the ruby?
Ans: The Prince sent the ruby to a poor seamstress whose son was ill and asking for oranges.
Q6: To whom did the Prince send his sapphire eyes?
Ans: He sent one sapphire to a playwright who was too cold to write, and the other to a match-girl who had dropped her matches in the gutter.
Q7: What happened to the Swallow in the end?
Ans: The Swallow died due to the extreme cold because he stayed back to help the blind Prince.
Q8: What happened to the leaden heart of the Prince?
Ans: The leaden heart snapped right in two when the Swallow died. It did not melt in the furnace later.
Q9: What did God ask his Angels to bring?
Ans: God asked his Angels to bring the two most precious things in the city.
Q10: What did the Angels bring to God?
Ans: The Angels brought the leaden heart of the Happy Prince and the dead bird.
Q11: Why did the Mayor call the statue shabby?
Ans: The statue had lost its gold, ruby, and sapphires, and looked dull and grey, so the Mayor called it shabby.
Q12: What is the moral of the story?
Ans: The moral is that true happiness lies in helping others and self-sacrifice. Love and compassion are the most precious things.
Q13: Why did the Swallow not go to Egypt?
Ans: He loved the Prince and could not leave him when he became blind, so he decided to stay and serve him.
Q14: How did the Swallow help the poor?
Ans: He flew over the city, saw the suffering of the poor, and distributed the gold leaves from the Prince's statue to them.
Q15: Who wrote 'The Happy Prince'?
Ans: Oscar Wilde.
