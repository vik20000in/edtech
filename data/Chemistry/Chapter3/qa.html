Matter - Questions and Answers

Q1: Define matter.
Ans: Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space.
Q2: What are the three states of matter?
Ans: Solid, Liquid, and Gas.
Q3: Why do solids have a fixed shape?
Ans: Solids have a fixed shape because the intermolecular forces of attraction are very strong, holding the particles tightly in fixed positions.
Q4: What is diffusion?
Ans: The intermixing of particles of two different substances on their own is called diffusion. It is fastest in gases.
Q5: Why can gases be compressed easily?
Ans: Gases can be compressed easily because there are large intermolecular spaces between their particles.
Q6: What is melting point?
Ans: The constant temperature at which a solid changes into a liquid at atmospheric pressure is called its melting point.
Q7: What is boiling point?
Ans: The constant temperature at which a liquid changes into a gas at atmospheric pressure is called its boiling point.
Q8: Define condensation.
Ans: The process of changing a gas into a liquid on cooling is called condensation.
Q9: What is freezing?
Ans: The process of changing a liquid into a solid on cooling is called freezing or solidification.
Q10: Why does the smell of hot food reach us from a distance?
Ans: The particles of hot food have high kinetic energy and diffuse faster into the air, reaching us quickly.
Q11: Differentiate between evaporation and boiling.
Ans: Evaporation is a surface phenomenon that occurs at any temperature below the boiling point. Boiling is a bulk phenomenon that occurs at a fixed temperature.
Q12: What is the kinetic theory of matter?
Ans: It states that matter is made of tiny particles which are in constant motion. The motion increases with temperature.
Q13: Why do liquids take the shape of the container?
Ans: Liquids have weaker intermolecular forces than solids, allowing particles to slide over each other, so they take the shape of the container.
