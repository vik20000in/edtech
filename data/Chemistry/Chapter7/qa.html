Physical and Chemical Changes - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a physical change?
Ans: A change in which no new substance is formed and the chemical properties remain the same is called a physical change. Example: Melting of ice.
Q2: What is a chemical change?
Ans: A change in which one or more new substances are formed with entirely different properties is called a chemical change. Example: Burning of paper.
Q3: Is rusting of iron a physical or chemical change?
Ans: Rusting of iron is a chemical change because a new substance (rust) is formed.
Q4: What are the conditions necessary for rusting?
Ans: Presence of oxygen (air) and moisture (water) are necessary for rusting.
Q5: How can rusting be prevented?
Ans: Rusting can be prevented by painting, oiling, greasing, or galvanization.
Q6: What is galvanization?
Ans: The process of depositing a layer of zinc on iron to prevent it from rusting is called galvanization.
Q7: What is crystallization?
Ans: The process of obtaining large crystals of a pure substance from its solution is called crystallization. It is a physical change.
Q8: Why is burning a candle both a physical and chemical change?
Ans: Melting of wax is a physical change, while burning of wax to produce heat and light is a chemical change.
Q9: What happens when vinegar reacts with baking soda?
Ans: Carbon dioxide gas is produced with a hissing sound. This is a chemical change.
Q10: Is souring of milk a physical or chemical change?
Ans: Souring of milk is a chemical change because a new substance (lactic acid) is formed and the change is irreversible.
Q11: What happens when magnesium ribbon is burnt?
Ans: It burns with a brilliant white light and forms a white ash called magnesium oxide. This is a chemical change.
Q12: Why is cutting wood a physical change but burning wood a chemical change?
Ans: Cutting wood only changes its size/shape (physical), but burning wood changes it into ash and smoke (chemical).
Q13: What is the chemical formula of rust?
Ans: Fe₂O₃.xH₂O (Hydrated Iron Oxide).
Q14: Is digestion of food a chemical change?
Ans: Yes, digestion is a chemical change because complex food substances are broken down into simpler substances by enzymes.
Q15: What indicates that a chemical change has taken place?
Ans: Evolution of gas, change in colour, formation of a precipitate, change in temperature, or production of sound/light indicate a chemical change.
