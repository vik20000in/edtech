Elements, Compounds and Mixtures - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is an element?
Ans: An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by any physical or chemical means. It is made of only one kind of atom.
Q2: What is a compound?
Ans: A compound is a pure substance formed when two or more elements combine chemically in a fixed ratio by mass.
Q3: What is a mixture?
Ans: A mixture is an impure substance formed when two or more substances are mixed together in any proportion without any chemical reaction.
Q4: Give the symbol for Gold, Silver, and Iron.
Ans: Gold: Au, Silver: Ag, Iron: Fe.
Q5: What is an atom?
Ans: An atom is the smallest particle of an element that may or may not exist independently but takes part in a chemical reaction.
Q6: What is a molecule?
Ans: A molecule is the smallest particle of an element or compound that can exist independently and retains the properties of that substance.
Q7: Differentiate between homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures.
Ans: A homogeneous mixture has a uniform composition throughout (e.g., salt solution). A heterogeneous mixture has a non-uniform composition (e.g., sand and water).
Q8: What is atomicity?
Ans: The number of atoms present in one molecule of an element is called its atomicity. For example, Oxygen (O₂) is diatomic.
Q9: Write the chemical formula for Water and Carbon Dioxide.
Ans: Water: H₂O, Carbon Dioxide: CO₂.
Q10: Is air a mixture or a compound? Why?
Ans: Air is a mixture because its components (nitrogen, oxygen, etc.) retain their individual properties and can be separated by physical means. They are not chemically combined.
Q11: What are metalloids?
Ans: Elements that show properties of both metals and non-metals are called metalloids. Examples: Silicon, Germanium.
Q12: Name a liquid metal and a liquid non-metal.
Ans: Liquid metal: Mercury. Liquid non-metal: Bromine.
Q13: How can you separate a mixture of iron filings and sulphur powder?
Ans: By using a magnet. Iron filings will be attracted to the magnet, leaving sulphur behind.
Q14: What is sublimation?
Ans: The process in which a solid directly changes into gas on heating without becoming liquid is called sublimation. Example: Camphor, Ammonium chloride.
Q15: What is filtration?
Ans: Filtration is a method used to separate insoluble solid particles from a liquid using a filter paper.
