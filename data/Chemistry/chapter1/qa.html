Chemistry: Introduction - Questions and Answers

Q: What is Science?

Science is the systematic effort by human beings to study, understand and utilise nature for meaningful purposes. This understanding is slowly developed by careful observations and experiments[cite: 287, 311].

Q: What is Chemistry?

Chemistry is the branch of science that deals with the study of the composition, physical and chemical properties of various forms of matter[cite: 288, 311].

Q: What are Fertilisers? Give examples.

Fertilisers are chemicals which provide nutrients to crops and increase their yield[cite: 289]. Examples include Urea, calcium nitrate, sodium nitrate, potash, and ammonium sulphate[cite: 290, 321].

Q: What are Pesticides? Give examples.

Pesticides are chemicals used to kill pests which affect the production of crops and fruits[cite: 290]. Examples include Aldrin, malathion, and parathion[cite: 291, 321].

Q: What are Fungicides? Give examples.

Fungicides are substances which protect the crops from fungi[cite: 292]. Examples include Bordeaux mixture and sulphur[cite: 292].

Q: What are Preservatives? Give examples.

Preservatives are chemicals which maintain the taste of food for a longer time and prevent degradation[cite: 300]. Examples include sugar, common salt, sodium benzoate, and sodium metabisulphate[cite: 301].

Q: What is a fuel? Give examples.

Fuels are substances which produce heat energy on burning[cite: 312]. Examples include L.P.G., Petrol, and Coal[cite: 302].

Q: How is chemistry helpful in improving human health?

Chemistry provides medicines like Antibiotics (e.g., Penicillin, Tetracycline), Pain killers, Vitamins, Enzymes, Minerals, and Anesthesia (e.g., chloroform) to cure diseases and improve health[cite: 313, 333].

Q: What was Alchemy?

Alchemy originated from the Greek word 'Khemeia' meaning "art of transmuting metals"[cite: 313]. It was partly based on experimentation and partly on spiritual discipline[cite: 314].

Q: What kind of experiments did Alchemists do?

Alchemists used general chemistry techniques in healing humans and contributed to future uses of chemicals, metals, ink, paints, cosmetics, medicines, etc[cite: 315, 316].

Q: What was the 'Philosopher's stone'?

It was a mythical, magical substance (wax, liquid, or powder) sought by alchemists, believed to turn base metals into gold and grant wealth, health, and immortality[cite: 316].

Q: What is the main difference between alchemy and chemistry?

Alchemy combined scientific and spiritual aspects and lacked a standardized scientific practice[cite: 317, 318]. Modern chemistry separated from these traditions, focusing on measurements and standardized methods[cite: 319, 337].

Q: How is chemistry important to mankind?

Chemistry provides things for daily use (toothpaste, soap, detergents, paints, clothes), medicines, agricultural chemicals (fertilisers, pesticides), plastics, fuels, consumer products (glass, paper), and defence materials (gunpowder, T.N.T.)[cite: 323, 324].

Q: What is the contribution of chemistry to Industry?

Chemistry improves efficiency and production in industries like metals, paints, paper, plastics, alloys, textiles, pharmaceuticals, electroplating, cosmetics, and synthetic fibres[cite: 326].

Q: What is the contribution of chemistry to Clothing?

Chemistry enables the conversion of natural fibres (cotton, jute, silk, wool) and the creation of synthetic fibres (nylon, terylene) into fabrics for various clothing and textile products[cite: 327, 328].

Q: What is the contribution of chemistry to National Defence?

Chemistry provides substances like gunpowder, T.N.T. (trinitrotoluene), phosgene, chemical weapons, and laughing gas used in national defence[cite: 330].

Q: Who is known as the Father of Modern Chemistry and why?

Robert William Boyle is known as the Father of Modern Chemistry[cite: 335]. He was the first to perform experiments under controlled conditions, publish detailed research, and put chemistry on a scientific footing based on measurements[cite: 336, 337]. He also defined elements, compounds, and mixtures[cite: 338].

Q: Who discovered Atoms?

John Dalton, a British chemist and physicist, proved that matter consists of small indivisible particles called 'atoms' and proposed the atomic theory[cite: 339, 340].

Q: Who discovered Oxygen?

Joseph Priestly discovered Oxygen[cite: 341].

Q: Who discovered the Safety Lamp?

Sir Humphry Davy discovered the Safety Lamp[cite: 341].
