Introduction to Chemistry - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is Chemistry?
Ans: Chemistry is the branch of science that deals with the study of the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter.
Q2: Who is known as the Father of Modern Chemistry?
Ans: Antoine Lavoisier is known as the Father of Modern Chemistry.
Q3: What is alchemy?
Ans: Alchemy was an ancient practice and a forerunner of chemistry, where alchemists tried to transform base metals like lead into gold and discover the elixir of life.
Q4: Name three main branches of Chemistry.
Ans: The three main branches are Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, and Physical Chemistry.
Q5: What is the Philosopher's Stone?
Ans: The Philosopher's Stone was a legendary substance that alchemists believed could turn cheap metals into gold and grant immortality.
Q6: What are apparatus?
Ans: The tools and equipment used in a chemistry laboratory to perform experiments are called apparatus.
Q7: What is the use of a test tube?
Ans: A test tube is used to hold, mix, or heat small quantities of chemicals.
Q8: What is a beaker used for?
Ans: A beaker is a cylindrical glass container used for holding liquids, mixing chemicals, and heating substances.
Q9: What is the function of a Bunsen burner?
Ans: A Bunsen burner is used for heating substances in the laboratory. It produces a hot, non-luminous flame.
Q10: Why is glass used to make laboratory apparatus?
Ans: Glass is used because it is transparent, easy to clean, withstands heat (especially Pyrex glass), and does not react with most chemicals.
Q11: What is a pipette used for?
Ans: A pipette is used to measure and transfer a specific volume of liquid accurately.
Q12: Who discovered Oxygen?
Ans: Joseph Priestley is credited with the discovery of Oxygen.
Q13: What is the scientific method?
Ans: The scientific method is a systematic way of learning about the world through observation, experimentation, and analysis.
Q14: Name a preservative used in food.
Ans: Sodium benzoate, salt, and vinegar are common preservatives used in food.
Q15: How has chemistry helped in agriculture?
Ans: Chemistry has provided fertilizers (like urea) to increase crop yield and pesticides to protect crops from pests.
