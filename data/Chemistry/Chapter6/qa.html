Acids, Bases and Salts - Questions and Answers

Q1: What are acids?
Ans: Acids are substances that taste sour and turn blue litmus red. Examples: Lemon juice (Citric acid), Vinegar (Acetic acid).
Q2: What are bases?
Ans: Bases are substances that taste bitter, feel soapy to touch, and turn red litmus blue. Examples: Soap, Baking soda.
Q3: What is an indicator?
Ans: An indicator is a substance that changes colour when added to an acidic or basic solution. Examples: Litmus, Turmeric, Phenolphthalein.
Q4: What is Litmus?
Ans: Litmus is a natural indicator extracted from lichens. It is available as a solution or as paper strips (red and blue).
Q5: What is neutralization?
Ans: The reaction between an acid and a base to form salt and water is called neutralization. Heat is also evolved. Acid + Base -> Salt + Water.

Q7: What is acid rain?
Ans: Rain containing excess acids (formed from pollutants like sulphur dioxide and nitrogen dioxide) is called acid rain.
Q8: Name the acid present in our stomach.
Ans: Hydrochloric acid (HCl) is present in our stomach. It helps in digestion.
Q9: What is used to treat acidity in the stomach?
Ans: Antacids like Milk of Magnesia (Magnesium hydroxide) are used to neutralize excess acid in the stomach.
Q10: What is the effect of Phenolphthalein on acids and bases?
Ans: Phenolphthalein is colourless in acidic solutions and turns pink in basic solutions.
Q11: Name the acid found in ant stings.
Ans: Formic acid is found in ant stings.
Q12: How can you treat an ant sting?
Ans: By applying a mild base like baking soda or calamine solution to neutralize the acid.
Q13: What are organic acids?
Ans: Acids that occur naturally in plants and animals are called organic acids. Examples: Citric acid, Lactic acid.
Q14: What are mineral acids?
Ans: Acids prepared from minerals of the earth are called mineral acids. Examples: Sulphuric acid, Hydrochloric acid.
Q15: What is the chemical name of common salt?
Ans: Sodium Chloride (NaCl).
