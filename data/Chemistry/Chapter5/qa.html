Air and Atmosphere - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is the atmosphere?
Ans: The blanket of air surrounding the earth is called the atmosphere.
Q2: What is the composition of air?
Ans: Air contains approximately 78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen, 0.9% Argon, 0.04% Carbon Dioxide, and small amounts of other gases, water vapour, and dust particles.
Q3: Which gas is essential for respiration and combustion?
Ans: Oxygen is essential for respiration and combustion (burning).
Q4: What is the role of Nitrogen in the atmosphere?
Ans: Nitrogen dilutes the activity of oxygen, preventing rapid burning. It is also essential for the growth of plants.
Q5: How is Carbon Dioxide useful?
Ans: Plants use Carbon Dioxide for photosynthesis to make food. It also keeps the earth warm by trapping heat (Greenhouse effect).
Q6: What are the layers of the atmosphere?
Ans: Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Thermosphere, and Exosphere.
Q7: In which layer of the atmosphere is the Ozone layer found?
Ans: The Ozone layer is found in the Stratosphere.
Q8: What is the function of the Ozone layer?
Ans: The Ozone layer protects us from the harmful ultraviolet (UV) rays of the sun.
Q9: What is air pollution?
Ans: The presence of harmful substances in the air that can damage the health of living beings and the environment is called air pollution.

Q11: What is the greenhouse effect?
Ans: The trapping of the sun's heat by gases like carbon dioxide and methane in the atmosphere, leading to a rise in earth's temperature, is called the greenhouse effect.
Q12: What is global warming?
Ans: The gradual increase in the average temperature of the earth's atmosphere due to the greenhouse effect is called global warming.
Q13: What is acid rain?
Ans: When gases like sulphur dioxide and nitrogen oxides react with rain water to form acids, it falls as acid rain. It damages buildings and crops.
Q14: How can we reduce air pollution?
Ans: By planting more trees, using cleaner fuels like CNG, and reducing the use of vehicles.
Q15: What is rust?
Ans: Rust is a reddish-brown flaky substance (hydrated iron oxide) formed on the surface of iron when it is exposed to moist air.
