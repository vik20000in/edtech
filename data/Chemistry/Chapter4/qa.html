Water - Questions and Answers

Q1: Why is water called a universal solvent?
Ans: Water is called a universal solvent because it can dissolve a wide variety of substances more than any other liquid.
Q2: What is the chemical formula of water?
Ans: The chemical formula of water is H₂O.
Q3: What are the three states in which water exists in nature?
Ans: Ice (solid), Water (liquid), and Water Vapour (gas).
Q4: Define the water cycle.
Ans: The continuous circulation of water from the earth's surface to the atmosphere and back to the earth is called the water cycle.
Q5: What is potable water?
Ans: Water that is fit for drinking is called potable water.
Q6: Differentiate between soft water and hard water.
Ans: Soft water lathers easily with soap (e.g., rainwater). Hard water does not lather easily with soap (e.g., sea water).
Q7: What causes hardness of water?
Ans: Hardness of water is caused by the presence of dissolved salts of calcium and magnesium (bicarbonates, chlorides, and sulphates).
Q8: How can temporary hardness be removed?
Ans: Temporary hardness can be removed by boiling the water.
Q9: What is a solute and a solvent?
Ans: The substance that dissolves is called the solute (e.g., salt). The liquid in which the solute dissolves is called the solvent (e.g., water).
Q10: What is a saturated solution?
Ans: A solution in which no more solute can be dissolved at a given temperature is called a saturated solution.
Q11: What is water pollution?
Ans: The contamination of water bodies with harmful substances is called water pollution.
Q12: Name two diseases caused by drinking polluted water.
Ans: Cholera and Typhoid.
Q13: What is conservation of water?
Ans: The careful and judicious use of water and preventing its wastage is called conservation of water.
Q14: What is rainwater harvesting?
Ans: The process of collecting and storing rainwater for future use is called rainwater harvesting.
Q15: At what temperature does water have maximum density?
Ans: Water has maximum density at 4°C.
