Nouns - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a noun?
Ans: A noun is a word used as the name of a person, place, animal, or thing.
Q2: What are the five kinds of nouns?
Ans: Proper Noun, Common Noun, Collective Noun, Material Noun, and Abstract Noun.
Q3: What is a Proper Noun?
Ans: A Proper Noun is the name of a particular person or place (e.g., Harry, London). It always begins with a capital letter.
Q4: What is a Common Noun?
Ans: A Common Noun is a name given in common to every person or thing of the same class or kind (e.g., boy, city, dog).
Q5: What is a Collective Noun?
Ans: A Collective Noun is the name of a number (or collection) of persons or things taken together and spoken of as one whole (e.g., crowd, team, flock).
Q6: What is an Abstract Noun?
Ans: An Abstract Noun is usually the name of a quality, action, or state considered apart from the object to which it belongs (e.g., kindness, laughter, childhood).
Q7: What is a Material Noun?
Ans: A Material Noun is the name of a substance or matter from which things are made (e.g., gold, wood, silver).
Q8: Differentiate between Countable and Uncountable Nouns.
Ans: Countable nouns are names of objects/people that we can count (e.g., book, apple). Uncountable nouns are names of things we cannot count (e.g., milk, sugar, honesty).
Q9: Give an example of a Collective Noun for ships.
Ans: A fleet of ships.
Q10: Give an example of a Collective Noun for cattle.
Ans: A herd of cattle.
Q11: Form an Abstract Noun from the adjective 'brave'.
Ans: Bravery.
Q12: Form an Abstract Noun from the verb 'know'.
Ans: Knowledge.
Q13: Is 'water' a Countable or Uncountable Noun?
Ans: Uncountable Noun.
Q14: Identify the noun type: 'The *jury* found the prisoner guilty.'
Ans: Jury is a Collective Noun.
Q15: Identify the noun type: 'Honesty is the best policy.'
Ans: Honesty is an Abstract Noun.
