Review Questions - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that makes complete sense, has a subject and a predicate, and begins with a capital letter.

Q2: Name the four types of sentences.

The four types of sentences are: Declarative (Statement), Interrogative (Question), Imperative (Command/Request), and Exclamatory (Strong feeling).

Q3: Give an example of a declarative sentence.

Example: The sun rises in the east.

Q4: Give an example of an interrogative sentence.

Example: Where do you live?

Q5: Give an example of an imperative sentence.

Example: Please close the door.

Q6: Give an example of an exclamatory sentence.

Example: What a beautiful garden!

Q7: How can you identify an interrogative sentence?

An interrogative sentence asks a question and ends with a question mark (?).

Q8: What punctuation mark is used at the end of an exclamatory sentence?

An exclamation mark (!) is used at the end of an exclamatory sentence.
