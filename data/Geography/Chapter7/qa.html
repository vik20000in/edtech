Human Environment - Questions and Answers

Q1: What are settlements?
Ans: Settlements are places where people build their homes.
Q2: Differentiate between temporary and permanent settlements.
Ans: Settlements occupied for a short time are temporary settlements. Settlements where people live for a long time are permanent settlements.
Q3: What are rural and urban settlements?
Ans: Rural settlements are villages where people are engaged in agriculture, fishing, etc. Urban settlements are towns and cities where people are engaged in manufacturing, trading, and services.
Q4: What are the four major means of transport?
Ans: Roadways, Railways, Waterways, and Airways.
Q5: What is the Golden Quadrilateral?
Ans: It is a network of highways connecting the four major metropolitan cities of India: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, and Kolkata.
Q6: Which is the fastest mode of transport?
Ans: Airways is the fastest mode of transport.
Q7: What are the two types of waterways?
Ans: Inland waterways (navigable rivers and lakes) and Sea routes (oceanic routes).
Q8: What is communication?
Ans: Communication is the process of conveying messages to others.
Q9: What is mass media?
Ans: Means of communication that can reach a large number of people at the same time, such as newspapers, radio, and television, are called mass media.
Q10: What is the Internet?
Ans: The Internet is a global computer network providing a variety of information and communication facilities.
Q11: What are compact settlements?
Ans: A compact settlement is a closely built area of dwellings, mostly found in flat land.
Q12: What are scattered settlements?
Ans: In a scattered settlement, dwellings are spaced over an extensive area. This is common in hilly tracts and thick forests.
Q13: Why are railways important?
Ans: Railways are important for carrying heavy goods and people over long distances quickly and cheaply.
Q14: What is the Trans-Siberian Railway?
Ans: It is the longest railway system connecting St. Petersburg in Western Russia to Vladivostok on the Pacific coast.
Q15: How have satellites helped in communication?
Ans: Satellites have made communication faster and helped in oil exploration, survey of forest, underground water, mineral wealth, weather forecast, and disaster warning.
