Agriculture - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is agriculture?
Ans: Agriculture is the science and art of cultivation on the soil, raising crops, and rearing livestock. It is also called farming.
Q2: What are the three types of economic activities?
Ans: Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary activities. Agriculture is a primary activity.
Q3: What is subsistence farming?
Ans: Farming practiced to meet the needs of the farmer's family with little or no surplus for sale is called subsistence farming.
Q4: What is commercial farming?
Ans: Farming done for sale in the market with the aim of making a profit is called commercial farming.
Q5: What is shifting cultivation?
Ans: A type of farming where a plot of land is cleared by felling and burning trees, cultivated for a few years, and then abandoned to regain fertility. Also known as 'Slash and Burn' agriculture.
Q6: What is plantation agriculture?
Ans: A type of commercial farming where a single crop like tea, coffee, sugarcane, or rubber is grown on a large scale.
Q7: Name the major food crops of the world.
Ans: Rice, Wheat, Maize, and Millets.
Q8: Which country is the leading producer of rice?
Ans: China is the leading producer of rice, followed by India.
Q9: What climatic conditions are required for growing wheat?
Ans: Wheat requires moderate temperature and rainfall during the growing season and bright sunshine at the time of harvest.
Q10: What is the Golden Fibre?
Ans: Jute is known as the Golden Fibre.
Q11: What is the Green Revolution?
Ans: The great increase in the production of food grains (especially wheat and rice) due to the introduction of high-yielding varieties, use of fertilizers, and better irrigation is called the Green Revolution.
Q12: What is organic farming?
Ans: A type of farming where organic manure and natural pesticides are used instead of chemicals. No genetic modification is done.
Q13: What is mixed farming?
Ans: A type of farming where the land is used for growing food and fodder crops and rearing livestock simultaneously.
Q14: Name the beverage crops.
Ans: Tea and Coffee.
Q15: What is sericulture?
Ans: Commercial rearing of silkworms for the production of silk is called sericulture.
