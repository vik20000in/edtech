Our Changing Earth - Questions and Answers

Q1: What are lithospheric plates?
Ans: The lithosphere is broken into a number of plates known as lithospheric plates. These plates move around very slowly.
Q2: Why do lithospheric plates move?
Ans: They move because of the movement of the molten magma inside the earth.
Q3: Differentiate between endogenic and exogenic forces.
Ans: Endogenic forces act in the interior of the earth (e.g., Earthquakes, Volcanoes). Exogenic forces work on the surface of the earth (e.g., Wind, Water).
Q4: What is a volcano?
Ans: A volcano is a vent (opening) in the earth's crust through which molten material erupts suddenly.
Q5: What is an earthquake?
Ans: The vibration of the earth's surface caused by the sudden movement of lithospheric plates is called an earthquake.
Q6: What is the focus and epicentre?
Ans: The place in the crust where the movement starts is called the focus. The place on the surface above the focus is called the epicentre.
Q7: How is an earthquake measured?
Ans: An earthquake is measured with a machine called a seismograph. The magnitude is measured on the Richter scale.
Q8: What is weathering?
Ans: Weathering is the breaking up of the rocks on the earth's surface.
Q9: What is erosion?
Ans: Erosion is the wearing away of the landscape by different agents like water, wind, and ice.
Q10: How are waterfalls formed?
Ans: When a river tumbles at a steep angle over very hard rocks or down a steep valley side, it forms a waterfall.
Q11: What are meanders?
Ans: As the river enters the plain, it twists and turns forming large bends known as meanders.
Q12: What is an ox-bow lake?
Ans: Due to continuous erosion and deposition along the sides of the meander, the ends of the meander loop come closer and eventually cut off from the river forming a cut-off lake, called an ox-bow lake.
Q13: What are sand dunes?
Ans: When the wind blows, it lifts and transports sand. When it stops blowing, the sand falls and gets deposited in low hill-like structures called sand dunes.
Q14: What is a delta?
Ans: A triangular landform formed at the mouth of a river where it deposits sediments before entering the sea is called a delta.
Q15: What are glacial moraines?
Ans: The material carried by the glacier such as rocks, sand, and silt gets deposited to form glacial moraines.
