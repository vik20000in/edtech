Water - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is a terrarium?
Ans: A terrarium is an artificial enclosure for keeping small house plants.
Q2: What is the water cycle?
Ans: The process by which water continually changes its form and circulates between oceans, atmosphere, and land is known as the water cycle.
Q3: What is the distribution of water on earth?
Ans: About 97% is saline water (oceans) and 3% is fresh water (ice caps, groundwater, lakes, rivers).
Q4: Why is ocean water salty?
Ans: Ocean water is salty because it contains large amounts of dissolved salts, mainly sodium chloride (common salt).
Q5: What are waves?
Ans: When the water on the surface of the ocean rises and falls alternately, they are called waves.
Q6: What is a tsunami?
Ans: A tsunami is a huge tidal wave caused by an earthquake, a volcanic eruption, or an underwater landslide.
Q7: What are tides?
Ans: The rhythmic rise and fall of ocean water twice in a day is called a tide.
Q8: What causes tides?
Ans: The strong gravitational pull exerted by the sun and the moon on the earth's surface causes tides.
Q9: Differentiate between Spring Tides and Neap Tides.
Ans: Spring Tides occur on full moon and new moon days when the sun, moon, and earth are in a line (highest tides). Neap Tides occur when the moon is in its first and last quarter (lowest tides).
Q10: What are ocean currents?
Ans: Ocean currents are streams of water flowing constantly on the ocean surface in definite directions.
Q11: Differentiate between warm and cold currents.
Ans: Warm currents originate near the equator and move towards the poles (e.g., Gulf Stream). Cold currents carry water from polar latitudes to tropical latitudes (e.g., Labrador Current).
Q12: Why are areas where warm and cold currents meet important?
Ans: These areas provide the best fishing grounds in the world. However, they also experience foggy weather making navigation difficult.
Q13: What is World Water Day?
Ans: World Water Day is celebrated on 22nd March to reinforce the need for water conservation.
Q14: What is precipitation?
Ans: Falling of moisture in the form of rain, snow, fog, sleet, or hailstone is called precipitation.
Q15: What is salinity?
Ans: Salinity is the amount of salt in grams present in 1000 grams of water. Average salinity of oceans is 35 parts per thousand.
