Air - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is the atmosphere?
Ans: Our earth is surrounded by a huge blanket of air called the atmosphere.
Q2: Which two gases make up the bulk of the atmosphere?
Ans: Nitrogen (78%) and Oxygen (21%).
Q3: What is the greenhouse effect?
Ans: Carbon dioxide traps the heat radiated from the earth and does not allow it to escape into space. This creates a greenhouse effect, keeping the earth warm.
Q4: What is global warming?
Ans: When the level of carbon dioxide increases in the atmosphere due to factory smoke or car fumes, the heat retained increases the temperature of the earth. This is called global warming.
Q5: Name the layers of the atmosphere.
Ans: Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Thermosphere, and Exosphere.
Q6: Which is the most important layer of the atmosphere?
Ans: The Troposphere is the most important layer because the air we breathe exists here, and almost all weather phenomena occur in this layer.
Q7: Where do aeroplanes fly?
Ans: Aeroplanes fly in the Stratosphere because it is free from clouds and associated weather phenomena.
Q8: What is weather?
Ans: Weather is the hour-to-hour, day-to-day condition of the atmosphere.
Q9: What is climate?
Ans: The average weather condition of a place for a longer period of time represents the climate of a place.
Q10: What is temperature?
Ans: The degree of hotness and coldness of the air is known as temperature.
Q11: What is insolation?
Ans: Insolation is the incoming solar energy intercepted by the earth.
Q12: What is air pressure?
Ans: Air pressure is defined as the pressure exerted by the weight of air on the earth's surface.
Q13: What is wind?
Ans: The movement of air from high pressure area to low pressure area is called wind.
Q14: Name the three types of winds.
Ans: Permanent winds (Trade winds, Westerlies), Seasonal winds (Monsoon), and Local winds (Loo).
Q15: What is humidity?
Ans: Moisture in the air at any time is known as humidity.
