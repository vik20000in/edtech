Inside Our Earth - Questions and Answers

Q1: What are the three layers of the earth?
Ans: Crust, Mantle, and Core.
Q2: What is the crust?
Ans: The uppermost layer over the earth's surface is called the crust. It is the thinnest of all layers.
Q3: What are the main mineral constituents of the continental mass?
Ans: Silica and Alumina. It is thus called 'sial' (si-silica and al-alumina).
Q4: What are the main mineral constituents of the oceanic crust?
Ans: Silica and Magnesium. It is therefore called 'sima' (si-silica and ma-magnesium).
Q5: What is the mantle?
Ans: The layer just beneath the crust is the mantle. It extends up to a depth of 2900 km.
Q6: What is the core?
Ans: The innermost layer of the earth is the core, with a radius of about 3500 km. It is mainly made of Nickel and Iron ('nife').
Q7: What is a rock?
Ans: Any natural mass of mineral matter that makes up the earth's crust is called a rock.
Q8: Name the three types of rocks.
Ans: Igneous rocks, Sedimentary rocks, and Metamorphic rocks.
Q9: How are igneous rocks formed?
Ans: When molten magma cools and becomes solid, igneous rocks are formed. They are also called primary rocks.
Q10: Differentiate between intrusive and extrusive igneous rocks.
Ans: Extrusive rocks are formed when molten lava comes on the earth's surface and cools rapidly (e.g., Basalt). Intrusive rocks are formed when magma cools deep inside the earth's crust (e.g., Granite).
Q11: How are sedimentary rocks formed?
Ans: Rocks roll down, crack, and hit each other to break into small fragments called sediments. These sediments are transported by wind/water and compressed to form sedimentary rocks (e.g., Sandstone).
Q12: How are metamorphic rocks formed?
Ans: Igneous and sedimentary rocks can change into metamorphic rocks under great heat and pressure (e.g., Clay into Slate, Limestone into Marble).
Q13: What is the rock cycle?
Ans: The process of transformation of the rock from one to another under certain conditions in a cyclic manner is known as the rock cycle.
Q14: What are minerals?
Ans: Minerals are naturally occurring substances which have certain physical properties and definite chemical composition.
Q15: Why is the core very hot?
Ans: The core is under immense pressure and contains radioactive elements, making it extremely hot.
