Our Environment - Questions and Answers

Q1: What is environment?
Ans: Everything that surrounds us—living and non-living things—is called our environment. It includes the place, people, things, and nature.
Q2: What are the components of the environment?
Ans: Natural (land, air, water, living things), Human-made (buildings, parks, bridges, roads), and Human (individual, family, community).
Q3: What is the lithosphere?
Ans: The solid crust or the hard top layer of the earth is called the lithosphere. It is made up of rocks and minerals.
Q4: What is the hydrosphere?
Ans: The domain of water is referred to as the hydrosphere. It comprises various sources of water like rivers, lakes, seas, oceans, etc.
Q5: What is the atmosphere?
Ans: The thin layer of air that surrounds the earth is called the atmosphere. It protects us from the harmful rays of the sun.
Q6: What is the biosphere?
Ans: The narrow zone of the earth where land, water, and air interact with each other to support life is called the biosphere.
Q7: What is an ecosystem?
Ans: A system formed by the interaction of all living organisms with each other and with the physical and chemical factors of the environment in which they live.
Q8: What is the biotic environment?
Ans: The world of living organisms, such as plants and animals, is called the biotic environment.
Q9: What is the abiotic environment?
Ans: The world of non-living elements, such as land, water, and air, is called the abiotic environment.
Q10: How do human beings modify the environment?
Ans: Humans modify the environment to fulfill their needs by clearing forests for agriculture, building dams, setting up industries, and constructing roads.
Q11: What is World Environment Day?
Ans: World Environment Day is celebrated on 5th June every year to raise awareness about environmental protection.
Q12: What is the barter system?
Ans: It is a trade in which goods are exchanged without the use of money.
Q13: Why is the lithosphere important?
Ans: It provides us with forests, grasslands for grazing, land for agriculture and human settlements, and is a source of mineral wealth.
Q14: What holds the atmosphere around the earth?
Ans: The gravitational force of the earth holds the atmosphere around it.
Q15: Give examples of human-made environment.
Ans: Buildings, bridges, roads, monuments, and industries.
